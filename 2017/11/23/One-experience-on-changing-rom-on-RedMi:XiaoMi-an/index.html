One experience on changing rom on RedMi/XiaoMi and Play Store Installation

Posted on 2017-11-23 | In others | Visitors

Change rom to the XiaoMi phone easily. Here are the steps.

  1. Prepare the usb or type c for your device, then connect it with your computer
  2. Shutdown your device and then press the power-off button and volume down key at the same time, after a while you will see the fastboot mode.
  3. Download the miflash-unlock tool to unlock your device. Then install it and open, following the prompts you will unlock the device.
  4. Download the XiaoMiflash.exe and the package of your device model fastboot
  5. Wait for a moment, you will see green success on the XiaoMiflash.

If you want to get the root after changing the rom, you should repeat the step three. Reboot your device, let your device run for a while (at least ten minutes) and then go to security -> manager apps -> permissions -> allow root access, check the box of the contract then downloading the root program. Finally you will get root access.

Happy Root.

###Install Google Play Store

There are two ways to install Google Play Store.

First and the easy one, you can download the google installers then get what you want.

Second, It’s complicated and hard to success because I often forgot to download Google Service Framework. The following content are quoted from APKMirror.com, hopes help.

To figure out the right version of Google Play services for your Android device, down Play Service info or go to Settings -> Apps -> Google Play services, and look at the 3-digit sequence (let’s use XYZ) in the parentheses:

  1. X defines Android version:
    • 0 for Android < 5.0
    • 2 for Android 5.0 and 5.1 starting with Play Servicces v8
    • 4 for Android 5.0 and 5.1 before Play Services v8, Android >= 6.0 after
    • 5 for Android Wear
    • 7 for Android 5.0
    • 8 for Android Tv
    • 9 for Android 8.0+
  2. Y defines CPU architecture:
    • 1 for armeabi
    • 3 for armeabi-v7a
    • 4 for arm64-v8a
    • 5 for mips
    • 7 for x86
    • 8 for x86_64
  3. Z defines DPI:
    • 0 for universal nodpi
    • 2 for 160
    • 4 for 240
    • 6 for 320
    • 8 for 480
      ​

Notes:

  • It seems that 7YZ builds became 4YZ with Google Play services v6.5. If you were on 7YZ before, you should now install 4YZ
  • It seems that 4YZ builds got split into 2YZ for Lollipop and 4YZ for Marshmallow with Google Play services v8. If you were on 4YZ before, you should now install 2YZ in Lollipop and 4YZ Marshmallow. Examples:
    • 014 for Android < 5.0 armeabi CPU 240 DPI device
    • 438 for Android 6.0+ armani-v7a CPU 480 DPI device
    • 876 for Android Tv x86 CPU 320 DPI device
    • 940 for Android 8.0 + arm64 CPU node device

I spent much time on changing rom to my device and install Google Play Store, So I hope this paper can make sense.
